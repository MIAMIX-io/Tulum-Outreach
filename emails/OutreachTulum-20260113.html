Lisbon, A European Gem

If you have a layover in Lisbon, our advice is clear: stay 2 or 3 nights to discover one of the most beautiful cities in Europe. Walking through its cobblestone streets that turn into stairways is an experience that transports you across centuries of history. The Portuguese capital perfectly blends its antiquity with a vibrant contemporary scene.

For lodging, Lisbon offers options for every taste. In Alfama, the oldest neighborhood, we recommend the Memmo Alfama Hotel with spectacular views of the Tagus River, or the boutique Santiago de Alfama for a more intimate experience. In Bairro Alto, the Bairro Alto Hotel combines contemporary design with a prime location, while in Chiado, the Almada Noire Boutique Hotel offers elegance and proximity to the best shops and restaurants.

British Airways Club Suite

Traveling from Buenos Aires to London always carries an air of excitement and anticipation. But doing so in Business Class with British Airways, aboard the modern Airbus A350-1000, transforms the journey into an experience of luxury and well-being. With a total duration of approximately 17 hours and 15 minutes, including a layover in Rio de Janeiro, the trip combines technology, elegance, and sustainability in perfect harmony.

Travel Guide 101

Follow Your Stomach to Local Markets

Action: Skip the restaurants right next to the major tourist sights. Instead, seek out a local wet market, farmers market, or even a large neighborhood grocery store.

For Planning & Booking Flights:

Skyscanner: Great for comparing flight prices across various airlines and finding the cheapest days to fly. Its "Everywhere" search feature is perfect for flexible travelers looking for a deal.

What’s Hot for 2026

  • Sri Lanka: Tourism is steadily rebuilding, offering incredible diversity (beaches, tea country, safaris, ancient ruins) in a compact, affordable package.
  • Morocco (Beyond Marrakech): New flight routes and hotel openings are making it more accessible. Travelers are exploring deeper, heading to the Atlas Mountains and the coastal cities beyond the famous souks.
  • Pantanal, Brazil: A major eco-tourism draw, emerging as the new must-do South American safari destination for spotting jaguars and diverse wildlife in the world's largest tropical wetland.
